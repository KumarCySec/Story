Her Kutty Kathai
She Never Planned to Tell

Without being asked, without a prompt — Harini sent a long message one evening. She called it a "kutty kathai" (a small story). It was the most intimate thing she'd ever shared about him.

"Kishore nu oru paiyan ta summa dhan pesa start pannen."
I started talking to Kishore just casually — no plan, no reason.
"One day I amazed to see — pesa start panna naal la irunthu daily uh pesirukom, expect 1 day — before Kerala porathuku munnadi varikum."
From the first day we started talking, we've spoken every single day — except one — right up until your Kerala trip.
"Apala avan enna velai irunthalu enkitta pesuvan. Content eh illa naalu pesuvan — athu pesurathula smiley dhan athigam irukum."
No matter how busy he is, he finds me. Even with nothing to say — most of what he sends is just smileys. But he sends them.
"Voluntary kekamaye help pannuvan. Enaku enna venum nu dhana understand panni pannuvan."
Without being asked, he helps. He somehow understands what I need before I say it.
"Ithula paakubothu enaku — epuraa ipdi oruthan apdi nu iruku."
When I look at all of this — I wonder, how is it that someone like him exists?
"Athula avan thappu ethum illa — na dhan ipdi maathiten."
None of this is his fault. I'm the one who changed — he stayed exactly who he was. I walked into my own feelings.

She sent this unprompted, on a quiet October evening. She called it a small story. He called it the most important thing he'd ever read. She blamed herself for catching feelings — as if it were a mistake. It wasn't.
